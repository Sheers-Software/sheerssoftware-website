# ICP Workflow & Pain Point Analysis

Based on the strategic documentation for Sheers Software's products, the company targets two distinct product categories with their respective Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs).

1. **Nocturn AI** (After-hours inquiry capture) targeting Independent 3-4 star hotels.
2. **Resort Genius** (AI agent-assist dashboard) targeting Hospitality Call Centers.

Below is the deep dive into the specific workflows, segment breakages, and specific pain points for each ICP.

---

## 1. Nocturn AI: Independent Hotel Operations (3-200 Rooms)

### ICP 1: The Reservation Manager (The User / Tier 1 Buyer)

**Daily Workflow Segments:**
1. **The Morning Onslaught (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM):** Arrives at work, opens WhatsApp Business, email, and the PMS. Confronted with 30-50 unread inquiries that arrived overnight.
2. **Manual Response & Triage (12:00 PM - 3:00 PM):** Replies manually to guests, looking up room rates, checking availability, answering FAQ ("Is parking free?"). Switching constantly between WhatsApp and the PMS.
3. **Follow-Up (3:00 PM - 5:00 PM):** Attempts to follow up with leads from previous days, though this is often skipped due to daily volume.
4. **The Shut Down (6:00 PM):** Leaves the office. The reservation channel is effectively closed for the next 15 hours.

**Workflow Breakages & Pain Points:**
*   **Segment 1 Breakage (The Overnight Leakage):** 100% breakage between 6PM and 9AM. The "Inquiry Graveyard". Leads arrive but get zero response. By the time the manager replies at 9:30 AM, the guest has already booked an alternative on Agoda.
*   **Segment 2 Breakage (Overwhelm & Inconsistency):** Triaging 50 messages creates extreme pressure. Quality of responses drops, typos occur, and complex inquiries are deprioritised.
*   **Segment 3 Breakage (The Follow-Up Failure):** No systematic way to track who hasn't replied yet. Follow-ups are reactive, leading to dropped balls.
*   **Exact Pain Point:** "I am the bottleneck." Constant performance anxiety. Feeling overwhelmed by the morning backlog. Facing angry guests who waited 12 hours for a reply. They don't want to be replaced; they want to feel like they have a "team of 5" helping them.

### ICP 2: The Revenue Manager (The Influencer / Tier 3 Buyer)

**Daily Workflow Segments:**
1. **Data Aggregation (Morning):** Pulls pickup reports from the PMS, checks Channel Manager for OTA performance.
2. **Strategy & Yield (Midday):** Adjusts rates based on occupancy trends and competitor pricing. Tries to formulate strategies to increase the share of direct bookings.
3. **Reporting (Afternoon):** Prepares weekly/daily RevPAR reports for the GM. 

**Workflow Breakages & Pain Points:**
*   **Segment 1 Breakage (The Attribution Black Box):** Cannot attribute a direct booking to specific marketing efforts because manual channels (WhatsApp/email) have zero analytics. They can only track website clicks, not actual WhatsApp conversion rates.
*   **Segment 2 Breakage (The OTA Trap):** Sees high OTA commission costs (15-25%) eating into profitability. They try to drive direct traffic, but because the reservations desk closes at 6PM, that traffic bounces instantly back to OTAs at night.
*   **Segment 3 Breakage (Unmeasurable Lead Value):** Cannot accurately measure the "Lost Opportunity Cost" of unanswered inquiries.
*   **Exact Pain Point:** High OTA commissions destroying yield. A total blind spot regarding direct inquiry conversion rates. They are expected to maximize revenue but lack the tools to plug the biggest leak: after-hours demand.

### ICP 3: The General Manager (The Economic Buyer / Tier 2 Buyer)

**Daily Workflow Segments:**
1. **Financial Review:** Reviews daily RevPAR, occupancy, and GOP (Gross Operating Profit) with the Revenue Manager. Answers to property owners.
2. **Resource Allocation:** Manages staffing budgets, handles hiring/turnover issues for front-of-house and reservations.
3. **Quality Assurance:** Reviews guest feedback and Tripadvisor complaints regarding service speed.

**Workflow Breakages & Pain Points:**
*   **Segment 1 Breakage (Cost vs. Coverage):** Cannot justify the RM 5,000+/month cost to hire a dedicated 24/7 night-shift reservations team. It destroys ROI.
*   **Segment 2 Breakage (The Vicious Cycle):** Implementing enterprise tech (like Opera integrations) takes 6-18 months and costs a fortune. Adopting generic chatbots usually fails because guests hate them and staff ignore them.
*   **Exact Pain Point:** Direct owner pressure on profitability. Knowing revenue is falling through the cracks after 6PM (RM 3,000 - 8,000/mo at risk) but having no cost-effective, easily implementable way to solve it.

## 2. Resort Genius: Hospitality Call Centers

### ICP 4: The Call Center Agent ("Sarah" - The User)

**Daily Workflow Segments:**
1. **Call Reception:** Answers 50+ calls a day from guests with a wide variety of unpredictable needs (from "Is the pool bar open?" to "Where is the nearest pharmacy?").
2. **Information Retrieval (The Search):** Puts the guest on hold. Rummages through 10 open browser tabs, thick binder manuals, scattered PDFs, and outdated intranet pages. 
3. **Resolution:** Relays the anwer to the guest, logs the call, wraps up.

**Workflow Breakages & Pain Points:**
*   **Segment 1 Breakage (Performance Anxiety):** Immediate stress when encountering "weird" or hyper-specific questions they haven't memorized.
*   **Segment 2 Breakage (Search Fatigue):** Spending 40% of their day just looking for information. The information is scattered, siloed, and often contradictory.
*   **Segment 3 Breakage (Metric Failure):** High Average Handle Time (AHT) metrics because they spend 8 minutes digging for an answer. 
*   **Exact Pain Point:** "Search Fatigue". The constant dread of being asked a question they don't know the answer to. The tension between wanting to be helpful and the pressure of strict AHT KPIs. They feel incompetent when they have to put guests on lengthy holds.

### ICP 5: The Operations Manager ("David" - The Buyer)

**Daily Workflow Segments:**
1. **KPI Monitoring:** Tracks department AHT, CSAT (Customer Satisfaction), and First Call Resolution rates.
2. **Training & QA:** Spends hours retraining staff, updating manual knowledge bases, auditing calls for quality.
3. **Hiring/Retention:** Constantly recruiting to backfill a leaky bucket of agents quitting due to stress.

**Workflow Breakages & Pain Points:**
*   **Segment 1 Breakage (The Onboarding Bottleneck):** It takes weeks to train a new agent to proficiency because there is too much institutional knowledge to memorize.
*   **Segment 2 Breakage (Inconsistent QA):** Different agents give different answers to the same questions because they are referencing different outdated PDFs.
*   **Segment 3 Breakage (The Churn Loop):** By the time an agent is finally profitable and proficient, they quit due to burnout. The cycle starts over.
*   **Exact Pain Point:** The Training Bottleneck. Being held accountable for AHT metrics that are structurally impossible to improve with their current scattered knowledge infrastructure. Endless hiring and training loops.
